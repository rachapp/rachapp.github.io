Core Research Areas

My research integrates computational physics, software engineering, and hands-on instrumentation to advance the capabilities of modern light sources.

Coherent Light Source Physics

My primary research involves the theoretical and computational study of Free-Electron Lasers (FELs). I develop and utilize advanced simulations to explore novel operational regimes, pushing the frontiers of coherent X-ray generation and understanding the complex physics that govern these powerful machines.

Scientific Software & Automation

I specialize in developing robust, Python-based software for scientific applications. This includes creating intuitive PyQt6 GUIs for beamline control, writing scripts for automated data acquisition, and building data processing pipelines to transform raw experimental data into meaningful scientific results.

Beamline Instrumentation & Development

A significant part of my work is the hands-on design, assembly, and commissioning of critical beamline hardware. This includes precision instruments like Double-Crystal Monochromators, motion control systems, and diagnostic tools that are essential for state-of-the-art synchrotron experiments.
