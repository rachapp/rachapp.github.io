My Research Interests

My research bridges experimental physics with computational analysis to solve challenges in materials science and beyond.

Synchrotron Techniques

My work focuses on leveraging X-ray Absorption Spectroscopy (XAS) and related techniques to probe the local atomic and electronic structure of advanced materials, particularly in the fields of catalysis and energy storage.

Computational Physics

I develop custom software and analysis scripts, primarily in Python, to process complex synchrotron data, automate experiments, and perform theoretical simulations to support experimental findings.

Instrumentation & Development

A significant part of my role involves the design, integration, and optimization of beamline components, including detectors, motion control systems, and data acquisition software to enhance experimental capabilities.
